An eraser is a tool used to remove marks made by pencils, pens, or chalk from paper. Erasers are also known as rubbers. How erasers work Erasers work by rubbing the graphite from pencil marks off of paper. Erasers are made from rubber, vinyl, plastic, or gum-like materials. Some erasers are attached to a pencil, while others can be temporarily removed and then added. Erasers produce some dust called debris. Types of erasers Less expensive erasers are made from synthetic rubber and synthetic soy-based gum. More expensive or specialized erasers are made from vinyl, plastic, or gum-like materials. Pink erasers are often used for normal papers, while blue erasers are for thick papers. White erasers can be used for all papers. How erasers are made Erasers are made by mixing an abrasive such as fine pumice with a rubbery matrix such as synthetic rubber or vinyl. The mixture is processed and extruded, and if made with rubber, vulcanized to bond the ingredients. A softener, such as vegetable oil, is also usually added to make the eraser more flexible.
